## Design Context

### Users

**Primary**: Travelers, truckers/logistics professionals, and construction/industry owners making safety-critical, time-sensitive decisions. They may be checking alerts on a job site, in a truck cab, or at a desk before a trip. They are practical, not technically specialized — they need to understand risk quickly and act confidently.

**Secondary**: General public seeking local environmental awareness — same need for clarity, lower stakes than the primary audience.

**Context of use**: Primarily desktop/laptop (web-first platform), but must work well on tablet and mobile since truckers and field workers check on devices. Users may be outdoors in variable lighting conditions, adding to the case for high contrast and WCAG AAA.

**Job to be done**: "Tell me if it's safe, how risky it is, and what I should do — in under 10 seconds."

---

### Brand Personality

**3 words**: Clean · Confident · Civilian-friendly

**Voice**: Calm authority without alarm-mongering. The product speaks like a trusted ranger or a knowledgeable friend — not a siren, not a bureaucratic form. It reassures through clarity, not minimization.

**Emotional goal**: Users leave feeling *prepared, informed, and confident*. Not anxious, not overwhelmed, not uncertain. The interface should never make them feel like they missed something important.

**What it is NOT**: Not militaristic/tactical (no dark greens, no aggressive typography). Not clinical/hospital (no sterile white-on-blue). Not playful/consumer (not an app for casual fun). Not alarmist (the visual language stays calm even when showing high-severity alerts).

---

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme**: Both light and dark mode supported. Light mode is primary.

**Light mode palette**: Cream base + Forest/Sage Green brand accent.
- Background: Warm cream (not pure white — keep the existing warmth around #fdf8f2)
- Brand accent: Forest green (oklch range ~0.52–0.58 L, 0.14–0.16 C, 145–155 H)
- Neutrals: Slightly green-tinted warm grays
- Alert severity colors (semantic, not brand): retain red/amber/green for critical/medium/low risk indicators — these are universal and must not change
- Retire coral (#ef6f51) as the primary brand accent; it may remain for specific alert-type indicators if needed

**Dark mode palette**: Deep warm dark backgrounds (not pure black — around oklch 0.18–0.22 L), same forest green accent, warm off-white text.

**Typography**:
- Headings: **Bricolage Grotesque** — a confident variable grotesque with humanist touches. Editorial authority without corporate coldness. Available on Google Fonts.
- Body: **Atkinson Hyperlegible** — purpose-built for legibility and accessibility. The WCAG AAA commitment makes this a natural pairing. Distinctive letterforms that signal "we take clarity seriously."
- Code/meta: Keep a monospace option but not IBM Plex Mono (reject list). Consider **Geist Mono** or **JetBrains Mono** for technical data displays.

**Visual character**:
- Clean grids with generous whitespace — not cramped data dashboards
- Environmental imagery through color, not illustration (the green palette does the work)
- Alert cards and severity indicators should be clear and immediate, never decorative
- Golby (AI assistant) lives as an embedded widget — unobtrusive but accessible, never interrupting the primary flow

---

### Design Principles

1. **Scannable in under 10 seconds** — the most critical risk information must be immediately visible without scrolling or clicking. Hierarchy is non-negotiable.

2. **Confidence through clarity** — no ambiguity in what a color, label, or indicator means. Every visual decision earns its place by reducing cognitive load, not adding visual interest.

3. **Environmental grounding** — the cream/green palette isn't decorative; it connects the interface to the natural world it monitors. Color reinforces meaning.

4. **Accessible by design, not by afterthought** — WCAG AAA (7:1 contrast for normal text, 4.5:1 for large text) is a baseline, not a stretch goal. Focus indicators, reduced motion support, and screen reader semantics are built in from the start.

5. **Embedded intelligence** — Golby is a helpful presence woven into the experience. It never takes over the screen or demands attention. It offers contextual guidance when the user needs it, stays quiet when they don't.
